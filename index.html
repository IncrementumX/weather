Executive Summary — what PWTS does & today's mandate (read-only)

PWTS monitors Polymarket weather markets, aligns each market to a forecast date and temperature threshold, estimates a model probability, compares it with the market-implied probability, then runs conservative safety gates.

Weather markets can be inefficient because they are specialized, time-sensitive, low-liquidity contracts. Retail pricing may lag forecast updates, station-bias corrections, market-date interpretation, or the actual YES/NO orderbook state.

Today the system is allowed to produce dry-run signals and diagnostics only. It is not allowed to place trades, submit orders, or turn recommendations into production execution.

Methodology · 8-step pipeline

Click to expand each layer

Eight discrete steps turn a Polymarket event into a gated signal. The summary list is always visible; expand a step to see what runs at that stage. Detailed gate-by-gate semantics live in Sub-tab 2 · 12 Gates below.

1–4 · Data layer — discovery, weather, date alignment, threshold parsing
1

Market discovery

Gamma weather events are scanned and filtered for tracked cities, temperature terms, valid end dates, condition IDs, and non-political/non-sports context.

2

Weather data

Open-Meteo forecasts are requested for the parsed market date and resolving-station coordinates when available. If a forecast is unavailable or stale, gates block the signal.

3

Market-date alignment

Signals parse the contract date from slug/question and avoid comparing a future market against today’s forecast.

4

Threshold parsing

Handles reach, exceed, above, over, at least, below, under, exact values, ranges, Fahrenheit, Celsius, and degree symbols.

5–8 · Model & sizing layer — probability, edge, CLOB, sizing
5

Probability model

The model uses station-targeted forecast temperature when available, applies station-city bias only for city-center fallback forecasts, and uses uncertainty/sigma to estimate contract probability.

6

Edge calculation

Edge is the model probability minus the executable market-implied probability for the evaluated side.

7

Side-aware CLOB

YES and NO books are distinct. NO signals use NO-side bid, ask, midpoint, spread, and slippage.

8

Sizing

Recommended size is an analytical dry-run output. Actual bet size remains zero until separate approval and operational work exists.

Gating / risk filters — what blocks a signal
  • Market date mismatch blocks forecasts that do not align with the contract date.
  • Stale data, unverified source, manual data, and unparsed market fields block signals.
  • City tier blocks: BLOCKED_EXCLUDED_CITY (hard; no station-bias data — currently only Beijing). YES-side discipline runs in a separate gate (yes_quality_gate) and may emit NO_TRADE_YES_QUALITY_LOW.
  • Missing volume, insufficient liquidity, high spread, high slippage, and partial-fill slippage block signals.
  • Duplicate signal checks, edge thresholds, size caps, and circuit breakers are part of the gate sequence.
City policy — see docs/CITY_POLICY.md
  • Active: Seoul, Chicago, New_York, Dallas, London, Seattle, Hong_Kong, Tokyo (and Denver, sample-limited). All have station_bias_db records and pass excluded_city_gate. Allocation eligible if all other gates pass.
  • Sample-limited (documented, not gated): Denver (n=14) and Hong_Kong (n=18) have small resolved-market samples. A future live-test phase would cap per-trade size at $2 for these until the sample grows.
  • YES-side discipline: only Chicago, Dallas, London, New_York, Seattle pass yes_quality_gate. Seoul, Denver, Hong_Kong, and Tokyo are NO-only.
  • Excluded: Beijing. No station_bias_db record. Gate returns BLOCKED_EXCLUDED_CITY.

Sub-tab 2 / 3 · 12 Gates explained

Every signal walks the 12-gate sequence in order. The first gate that triggers wins and stamps a status; later gates are not evaluated. Gates marked kill (1, 11, 12) can hard-stop regardless of edge. Gate 7 adaptive relaxes its thresholds when edge is high. Source of truth: scripts/gates.py.

1paper_to_live_gate
Hard kill switch. Any attempt to flip the pipeline out of dry-run is blocked unless the LIVE env vars and approval handshake are present.
Trigger: dry_run = False · Status: BLOCKED_NEEDS_APPROVAL
2market_source_gate
Rejects any market that did not come from the verified Gamma + CLOB read path (no manual market data, no market_universe.json).
Trigger: source_api ≠ "gamma+clob" · Status: BLOCKED_MANUAL_MARKET_DATA
3staleness_gate
Forces a hard freshness contract on both odds and the weather forecast snapshot — no signal off a half-hour old quote and a four-hour old forecast.
Trigger: odds or weather older than 180 min · Status: BLOCKED_STALE_DATA
4excluded_city_gate
Beijing is hard-excluded (no station_bias_db record). Any other city without bias data is also blocked. YES-side discipline runs in yes_quality_gate.
Trigger: missing station-bias record · Status: BLOCKED_EXCLUDED_CITY / BLOCKED_NO_BIAS_DATA
5weather_data_gate
Open-Meteo failure or unparsed forecast on the parsed market date short-circuits the signal — model_prob cannot be computed without temperature.
Trigger: Open-Meteo status = FAILED · Status: BLOCKED_NO_WEATHER_DATA
6market_already_ended
A 2h end-of-life buffer keeps the system from scoring markets that have resolved or are about to lock — protects against post-resolution liquidity traps.
Trigger: endDate < now − 2h · Status: market_already_ended
7liquidity_gate
Adaptive: at edge ≥ 15% the floor relaxes from $5K/$2.5K to $1.5K/$750 because the edge cushions slippage. Today this is the dominant block (42/55 signals).
Trigger: vol/OI < (edge≥15%: $1.5K/$750, else $5K/$2.5K) · Status: BLOCKED_NO_LIQUIDITY
8spread_slippage_gate
Caps execution friction so the edge is not erased on entry. Slippage is measured for a $10 round-trip on the side being signaled (YES or NO book).
Trigger: spread > 0.05 or slippage > 0.02 · Status: BLOCKED_SPREAD
9duplicate_run_gate
Idempotency check inside a single trading day. Prevents the same market_id + date from emitting two paper orders if the pipeline runs twice.
Trigger: same market_id+date already signaled · Status: BLOCKED_DUPLICATE
10edge_gate
Minimum disagreement floor between the model and market. Below this, the system cannot distinguish noise from signal and emits NO_TRADE_EDGE_TOO_LOW.
Trigger: |model − market| < 8% · Status: NO_TRADE_EDGE_TOO_LOW
11size_gate
Position cap: recommendation outside $1–$10 is rejected as a safety bound. Anything that would size beyond this requires Eduardo approval.
Trigger: recommended size > $10 or < $1 · Status: BLOCKED_NEEDS_APPROVAL
12circuit_breaker_gate
Read from calibration_v2_rigorous.json. If rolling Brier or skill score degrades, the entire system halts new entries until recalibrated.
Trigger: Brier > 0.20, skill < 0, or UNKNOWN · Status: BLOCKED_CIRCUIT_BREAKER

Sub-tab 3 / 3 · Edge & Sizing

Edge is the gap between what the model thinks will happen and what the market is pricing. PWTS computes it on the YES side; for a NO bet, the trader-facing edge is the magnitude flipped. The edge is the entry-quality signal, but the gates — not the edge — decide whether a position is actionable.

edge = model_probability(YES) − market_probability(YES)
net_edge = edge − fee_rate_assumption (≈ 2%) − expected_slippage
Actionable when |edge| ≥ 8% (edge_gate), liquidity passes, spread < 5%, slippage < 2%, and the city/side passes calibration. The sign of edge tells you the side: positive favors YES, negative favors NO.

Sizing formula — how edge becomes a size
conviction_score ∈ [0, 1] ← weighted blend of side prior, city prior, model confidence, execution quality, liquidity, source verification, weather status
conviction_cap = 0 if conviction < 0.55, else {2%, 4%, 7%, 10%} of bankroll by tier (0.55 / 0.65 / 0.75 / 0.85)
liquidity_cap = min(0.5% · volume, 0.5% · open_interest)
side_cap = 6% of bankroll if YES, else $10 hard cap
slippage_factor = 1 − min(slippage / 2%, 1) · 0.75
size = min($10, side_cap, conviction_cap, liquidity_cap) · slippage_factor / √correlated_count
Final clamp: size ≥ $1 to emit a recommendation, ≤ $10 hard cap. Source: scripts/gates.py::compute_conviction_bet_size. Actual bet_size_usd remains $0.00 in DRY-RUN regardless of recommended_bet_size_usd.

Sizing Model for US$100 Bankroll

Use conviction as the primary sizing signal, then haircut by slippage, liquidity, side quality, and portfolio concentration. Edge remains a gate and sanity check, not the main size dial.

Formula adopted for handoff — mirror of Sub-tab 3
  • Conviction score: weighted blend of side prior, city prior, model confidence, execution quality, liquidity, source verification, and weather status.
  • Conviction cap: 0% below 0.55, then 2%, 4%, 7%, or 10% of bankroll by confidence tier.
  • Liquidity cap: min(0.5% of volume, 0.5% of open interest).
  • Slippage haircut: size is reduced as 10-dollar slippage approaches the 2% gate.
  • Side cap: NO can use the normal cap; YES is capped at 6% of bankroll until YES hit ratio improves.
  • Final recommendation: min of absolute cap, side cap, conviction cap, and liquidity cap, then slippage/correlation adjusted.
